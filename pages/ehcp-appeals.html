EHCP Appeals & SEND Tribunal Support

EHCP refused? Local authority won't assess? EHC Plan content wrong? Our experienced SEN advocates help you challenge incorrect decisions with expert education advocacy and proven appeal strategies.

Understanding Your Right to Appeal an EHCP Decision

Under the Children and Families Act 2014, parents and young people have the right to appeal certain decisions made by local authorities about Education, Health and Care Plans (EHCPs) to the First-tier Tribunal (Special Educational Needs and Disability) — commonly known as the SEND Tribunal or SENDIST.

In 2025, SEND tribunal appeals rose by 55%, with over 21,000 appeals registered. Families won in approximately 95% of cases, while local authorities succeeded in just 1.3% — the lowest on record. This extraordinary success rate demonstrates that local authorities frequently make incorrect decisions — and that challenging those decisions with the right education advocacy works.

Our experienced SEN advocates help you navigate the EHCP appeal process with confidence, preparing robust cases that give your child the best chance of receiving the specified and quantified provision they need and are entitled to by law. Whether your child has autism, ADHD, dyslexia, or any other special educational need, we have the SEND legal expertise to support your appeal.

Types of EHCP Appeals We Handle

Refusal to Assess Appeals

If your local authority refuses to carry out an EHC needs assessment (EHCNA), you have the right to appeal within two months of the decision letter (or one month from your mediation certificate, whichever is later). Under Section 36 of the Children and Families Act 2014, local authorities must agree to assess if there is reason to believe a child may have SEN and may need an EHCP — a relatively low threshold that is often incorrectly applied, particularly for children with autism, ADHD, or less visible needs.

What we do:

  • Review the refusal decision and advise on merits
  • Prepare all documents for tribunal registration
  • Advise on evidence gathering and legal arguments
  • Liaise with the Tribunal, school, and local authority
  • Provide ongoing advice until the appeal concludes

EHCP Refusal Appeals

If the local authority conducts an EHC needs assessment but then refuses to issue an EHCP, you can appeal this decision to the SEND Tribunal. This often happens when the authority claims the child's needs can be met through existing SEN support and the school's graduated approach — even when the evidence from educational psychologists, speech and language therapists, or other professionals suggests otherwise.

What we do:

  • Analyse the assessment reports and refusal reasoning
  • Prepare all documents required for the appeal
  • Advise on the law and make recommendations on evidence and tactics
  • Provide professional support throughout the appeal process
  • Negotiate with the local authority where appropriate

EHCP Content Appeals

Even once an EHC Plan is issued, it may not accurately describe your child's special educational needs or specify the right provision. Under SEND law, provision in Section F must be specific, detailed, and quantified. You can appeal Sections B (special educational needs), F (SEN provision), and I (school placement) of the plan to the SEND Tribunal, and you can also extend the appeal to include health and social care aspects.

What we do:

  • Conduct detailed analysis of the EHCP against legal requirements
  • Draft comprehensive Grounds of Appeal
  • Advise on and draft amendments to the Working Document
  • Review and advise on expert reports
  • Support through to Final Hearing Bundle deadline

The EHCP Appeal Process — Step by Step

Understanding the timeline and key stages of a SEND Tribunal appeal, from obtaining your mediation certificate to the final hearing.

1

Mediation Certificate

Before you can register your appeal, you must either attend SEND mediation or obtain a mediation certificate confirming you've contacted the mediation service. This is a legal requirement — you cannot lodge an appeal without it. Our SEN advocates guide you through this process.

2

Register Your Appeal

The appeal must be registered with the SEND Tribunal using the SEND35 form within two months of the local authority's decision letter (or one month from the mediation certificate date, whichever is later). Our education advocates prepare and submit everything for you.

3

Case Preparation

We gather evidence (including educational psychologist reports and independent assessments), draft comprehensive Grounds of Appeal, prepare the Working Document, review expert reports, and build your case over the following weeks. This is where thorough preparation by an experienced SEN advocate makes the difference.

4

Hearing & Resolution

Many cases settle before reaching a full hearing through negotiation with the local authority. If yours goes to tribunal, our educational advocates can represent you in person at the First-tier Tribunal. The Tribunal panel makes a legally binding decision that the local authority must implement — there are legal consequences if they don't comply.

Key Facts About SEND Tribunal Appeals

95% Win Rate

Parents who appeal to the SEND Tribunal win in approximately 95% of cases, reflecting the frequency of incorrect LA decisions.

20+ Week Timeline

From lodging an appeal to a hearing typically takes around 20 weeks, though many cases settle earlier through negotiation.

No Cost Order Risk

The Tribunal does not normally make cost orders, meaning you won't be asked to pay the local authority's costs if your appeal is unsuccessful.

Legally Binding

Tribunal decisions are legally binding on the local authority. If you win, the LA must implement the Tribunal's order — there are legal consequences if they don't.
