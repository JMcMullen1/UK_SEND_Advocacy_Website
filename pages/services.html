EHCNA Requests

An Education, Health and Care Needs Assessment (EHCNA) is the essential first step in obtaining an EHCP for your child under the Children and Families Act 2014. The strength of the initial request can determine whether your local authority agrees to assess — particularly for children with autism, ADHD, dyslexia, or other special educational needs where schools may claim needs can be met through SEN support alone.

We help you draft a compelling and comprehensive EHCNA request that clearly demonstrates your child's needs, referencing the relevant legal tests under Section 36 of the Children and Families Act and supporting evidence to maximise the chances of success.

  • Review of existing reports and evidence
  • Drafting a legally robust EHCNA request letter
  • Guidance on gathering supporting evidence
  • Follow-up support and next steps advice

Refusal to Assess Appeals

If your local authority refuses to carry out an EHC needs assessment, you have the right to appeal to the First-tier Tribunal (Special Educational Needs and Disability) — commonly known as the SEND Tribunal. Local authorities must agree to assess where there is reason to believe a child may have SEN and may need an EHCP. Our SEN advocates can help you exercise that right effectively.

Our education advocates prepare all necessary documents to submit the appeal using the SEND35 form, including reviewing and advising on SEND law, evidence, and tactics. We provide continued advice and support throughout the appeal process, liaising with the Tribunal, your child's school, and the local authority.

  • Full preparation of appeal documentation
  • Legal advice on merits and strategy
  • Liaison with Tribunal, school, and local authority
  • Ongoing support until resolution

EHCP Content Appeals

If your child has an EHC Plan but it doesn't accurately describe their needs or the provision required, you can appeal the content of Sections B (special educational needs), F (SEN provision), and I (school placement) to the SEND Tribunal. Getting the detail right is crucial — provision must be specified and quantified, and it determines the support your child receives every day.

We prepare all documents required to submit the appeal, including drafting comprehensive Grounds of Appeal and advising on the Working Document process, with continued education advocacy support up to the date the Final Hearing Bundle is due.

  • Detailed analysis of EHCP against legal requirements
  • Drafting comprehensive Grounds of Appeal
  • Working Document amendments and advice
  • Support through to Final Hearing Bundle

Tribunal Representation

When your case reaches the SEND Tribunal, having an experienced educational advocate by your side can make all the difference. Parents win 95% of appeals at the First-tier Tribunal — and expert representation helps ensure your case is presented as strongly as possible. We provide in-person attendance and representation at tribunal hearings across England.

Our SEN advocates present your case with authority, cross-examine local authority witnesses effectively, and ensure your child's educational rights are upheld where it matters most. We handle all preparation in the lead-up to the hearing date, including reviewing the Final Hearing Bundle.

  • Full hearing preparation and case presentation
  • Expert cross-examination of LA witnesses
  • In-person attendance at hearings nationwide
  • Post-hearing follow-up and compliance
